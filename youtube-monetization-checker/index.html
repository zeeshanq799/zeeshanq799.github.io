YouTube Monetization Requirements 2026

To join the YouTube Partner Program and start earning ad revenue, your channel must meet one of two eligibility paths:

‚úÖ Path 1: Long-Form Monetization

Requirements:

  • 1,000 subscribers
  • 4,000 public watch hours in the past 12 months

Timeline: Most creators reach this in 6-18 months with consistent uploads.

‚úÖ Path 2: Shorts Monetization

Requirements:

  • 1,000 subscribers
  • 10 million public Shorts views in the past 90 days

Important clarifications:

  • Both subscriber AND watch hour/Shorts view requirements must be met simultaneously
  • Only public watch time counts (not private or unlisted videos)
  • YouTube uses rolling 12-month (long-form) or 90-day (Shorts) windows
  • Shorts watch time does NOT count toward the 4,000-hour requirement

How to Check Your YouTube Watch Hours

YouTube Studio shows your exact monetization progress:

  1. Go to YouTube Studio (studio.youtube.com)
  2. Click Analytics in the left sidebar
  3. Select the Monetization tab
  4. View your current watch hours and subscriber count

The dashboard displays:

  • 12-month watch hours: Rolling total from past 365 days
  • Subscriber count: Current total subscribers
  • Progress bars: Visual representation of both requirements
  • Estimated eligibility: When you might qualify (if growth continues)

This data updates daily, so you can track progress in real-time.

Long-Form vs Shorts: Which Path Is Faster?

Both paths have advantages depending on your content style:

Long-Form Path (4,000 Hours)

Advantages:

  • More achievable for most creators (48,000 views at 5-min retention)
  • Higher ad revenue potential (RPM $2-$12 vs Shorts $0.05)
  • Builds sustainable channel foundation
  • Better audience retention and loyalty

Best for: Tutorials, vlogs, reviews, educational content

Shorts Path (10M Views)

Advantages:

  • Can accumulate views faster due to algorithm distribution
  • Easier to produce (15-60 seconds vs 8+ minutes)
  • Lower barrier to viral growth
  • Good for trend-based content

Best for: Dancing, comedy skits, quick tips, viral trends

Reality check: While 10 million sounds like a lot, Shorts can accumulate views quickly. However, long-form content generates 20-80x more revenue per view after monetization. Most creators pursue long-form for better income potential.

Common Mistakes That Delay Monetization

Avoid these errors that slow your progress:

  • Uploading Shorts to reach 4,000 hours: Shorts watch time doesn't count toward long-form requirement. Focus on videos longer than 60 seconds.
  • Sub4Sub tactics: Fake engagement violates YouTube policies and doesn't generate watch time. Can result in channel termination.
  • Buying views/watch time: YouTube detects artificial engagement. Can result in permanent monetization ban.
  • Uploading copyrighted content: Copyright strikes disqualify you from monetization and can't be removed for 90 days.
  • Inconsistent uploads: Uploading 10 videos one month, then nothing for 3 months confuses the algorithm and viewers. Consistency matters more than volume.
  • Ignoring audience retention: 100 videos with 2-minute retention won't get you to 4,000 hours. Focus on improving watch time, not just views.

What Happens After You're Approved?

Once you meet requirements and apply:

  1. Application review: YouTube reviews your channel (1-4 weeks typically)
  2. Policy check: Verify compliance with community guidelines and advertiser-friendly content guidelines
  3. Approval notification: You'll receive email notification when approved
  4. AdSense linking: Connect or create AdSense account for payment processing
  5. Ads enabled: YouTube places ads on your videos automatically
  6. First payment: Earn at least $100 to trigger first payout (typically 21st-26th of each month)
