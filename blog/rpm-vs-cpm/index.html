If you're monetizing on YouTube, understanding the difference between RPM (Revenue Per Mille) and CPM (Cost Per Mille) is critical.

Many creators focus on CPM because it looks impressive. But CPM does not reflect what you actually earn.

Simple breakdown:
CPM = What advertisers pay.
RPM = What you actually earn per 1,000 total views.

What Is CPM? (Cost Per Mille)

CPM represents the amount advertisers pay for 1,000 ad impressions on your videos.

Important details:

  • Calculated before YouTube‚Äôs revenue share
  • Only applies to monetized ad impressions
  • Reflects advertiser demand in your niche

A high CPM usually indicates a high-value niche such as finance, business, or technology.

What Is RPM? (Revenue Per Mille)

RPM measures your total earnings per 1,000 views ‚Äî after YouTube takes its cut.

RPM includes:

  • Ad revenue (after 45% share)
  • Channel memberships
  • Super Chat & Super Thanks
  • YouTube Premium revenue

RPM Formula:
RPM = (Total Revenue √∑ Total Views) √ó 1000

This is the metric that reflects real monetization performance.

  1. YouTube takes 45% of ad revenue.
  2. Not every view shows an ad.
  3. RPM includes all views ‚Äî even unmonetized ones.

Which Metric Should You Focus On?

Focus on RPM.

CPM tells you how attractive your niche is to advertisers. RPM tells you how effectively your content converts attention into income.

If CPM is high but RPM is low, possible issues include:

  • Low audience retention
  • Poor mid-roll ad placement
  • High percentage of unmonetized views
  • Weak monetization diversification

How to Increase RPM in 2026

  • Create longer content eligible for mid-roll ads
  • Improve audience retention rate
  • Target Tier-1 countries (US, UK, Canada, Australia)
  • Maintain advertiser-friendly content
  • Add memberships and fan funding options
  • Combine YouTube income with affiliate marketing and AdSense monetization

Quick Summary: RPM vs CPM in One Paragraph

CPM (Cost Per Mille) measures how much advertisers pay per 1,000 monetized ad impressions before YouTube takes its cut. RPM (Revenue Per Mille) measures how much a creator actually earns per 1,000 total views after YouTube's revenue share and including all monetization sources. For income optimization, RPM is the metric that matters.

CPM measures advertiser demand.
