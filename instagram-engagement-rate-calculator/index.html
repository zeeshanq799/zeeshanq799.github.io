How Brands Calculate Engagement

Brand evaluation process for sponsorships:

Step 1: Average Engagement Rate

Brands analyze last 10-30 posts to calculate average engagement rate. They ignore outliers (viral posts or unusually low posts) to find consistent baseline.

Step 2: Engagement Quality

Not all engagement is equal. Brands weight actions differently:

  • Saves: Highest value (intent to reference later)
  • Shares: High value (organic reach extension)
  • Comments: Medium-high value (active interaction)
  • Likes: Medium value (passive acknowledgment)

Step 3: Audience Authenticity

Brands audit for fake followers using tools like HypeAuditor or Social Blade:

  • Sudden follower spikes (buying followers)
  • High follower count with low engagement (fake followers)
  • Generic/bot comments ("Nice post!", "üòçüòçüòç")
  • Follower demographics mismatch with content

Step 4: Sponsorship Multiplier

How engagement affects sponsorship rates:

Engagement Rate Pricing Impact

  • >6% engagement: Charge 20-40% premium above base rate
  • 3-6% engagement: Standard market rates apply
  • <3% engagement: Expect 10-20% discount requests
  • <1% engagement: Brands may decline or request steep discounts

Example: Creator with 50K followers and 5% engagement can charge $750-$1,200 per post. Same follower count with 1.5% engagement: $400-$600 per post.
